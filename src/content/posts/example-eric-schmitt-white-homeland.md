## The Statement

At the 2025 National Conservatism Conference, Senator Eric Schmitt declared:

> "This is our home. This is a white homeland... We are the sons and daughters of the Christian pilgrims that poured out from Europe's shores."
>
> — Eric Schmitt, National Conservatism Conference, September 2025

*Source: Missouri Independent, September 15, 2025*

---

## Claim-by-Claim Fact Check

| Claim | Verdict |
|-------|---------|
| America is a "white homeland" | **FALSE** — America has been multiracial since before its founding. Indigenous peoples lived here for thousands of years before European contact. Enslaved Africans were present from the 1600s. The Constitution makes no reference to race as a basis for national identity. |
| Americans are "sons and daughters of Christian pilgrims from Europe" | **FALSE** — As of 2025, ~40% of Americans are non-white. Millions of Americans descend from enslaved Africans, Indigenous peoples, Asian immigrants, Latin American immigrants, and non-Christian European immigrants. |

## Analysis

### Factor 1: Factual Accuracy — 5%

This is not a matter of interpretation or spin. The claim that America is a "white homeland" is factually wrong on every level:

- **Demographically:** The U.S. population in 2025 is approximately 58% non-Hispanic white, 19% Hispanic, 13% Black, 6% Asian, and 1.3% Native American. Roughly 4 in 10 Americans are not white.

- **Historically:** Indigenous peoples inhabited this land for 15,000+ years before European contact. Enslaved Africans were brought to Jamestown in 1619. The country was built in large part by non-white labor.

- **Legally:** The 14th Amendment guarantees equal protection regardless of race. The Civil Rights Act of 1964 prohibits racial discrimination. No founding document defines America as a white nation.

- **Constitutionally:** The Constitution's Preamble says "We the People" — not "we the white people" or "we the Europeans."

The only way this claim gets any points at all is that European settlers did found the colonial governments. But describing the entire nation as a "white homeland" based on colonial history is like describing a company by its founding investors while ignoring everyone who built it.

**Rating: Pants on Fire**

### Factor 2: Intent to Mislead — 10%

This is where the score could actually be debated — but not in the direction Schmitt would prefer. There are two possibilities:

1. **He believes it.** In which case this is a sincere expression of white supremacist ideology from a sitting U.S. Senator.

2. **He's performing it.** In which case he's deliberately invoking white nationalist rhetoric to appeal to that base at a conference known for its hard-right attendees.

Either way, a Yale-educated attorney and former state Attorney General knows the demographics and history of the United States. He is not making an accidental error. He is either expressing genuine white supremacist belief or deliberately deploying supremacist rhetoric for political gain.

His silence when a fight over Nazis and antisemitism erupted within the GOP two months later (November 2025) further suggests this is a calculated posture, not a gaffe.

**Rating: Blatant Deception / Genuine Belief in False Premise**

### Factor 3: Context & Cherry-Picking — 10%

Schmitt's speech cherry-picks a single strand of American history (European colonial settlement) and presents it as the entirety of the American story:

| What Schmitt Included | What Schmitt Left Out |
|----------------------|----------------------|
| European Christian settlers | Indigenous peoples who lived here first |
| "Pilgrims that poured out from Europe" | Enslaved Africans brought here by force |
| White identity as American identity | Every non-white American citizen |
| Colonial founding narrative | 250 years of immigration from every continent |

This is not cherry-picking in the normal political sense (emphasizing favorable data). This is erasing the existence of roughly 130 million Americans.

**Rating: Severe Cherry-Picking**
