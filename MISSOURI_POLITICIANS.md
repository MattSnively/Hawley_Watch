## #1: Eric Schmitt — U.S. Senator

**Party:** Republican
**In office since:** 2023
**Previous role:** Missouri Attorney General
**National profile:** High — MAGA media regular, led Murthy v. Missouri censorship case

### Why He's the Top Candidate

Schmitt is the closest analog to Hawley in Missouri politics. Extremely active on X/Twitter, regularly makes claims fact-checkers have rated false or misleading, and has a significant gap between his stated principles (free speech champion, populist) and his actions (white supremacist rhetoric, dark money backing).

### Key Controversies

- **White homeland speech (2025):** At the National Conservatism Conference, Schmitt declared America a "white homeland" and described Americans as "the sons and daughters of the Christian pilgrims that poured out from Europe's shores," erasing Indigenous, Black, and immigrant contributions. He then went silent when a fight over Nazis and antisemitism roiled the GOP.
- **Repeated false claims:** As AG, he wrote a letter to Biden containing a factually false claim about Biden's travel record. CNN fact-checked it as definitively wrong.
- **Great Replacement rhetoric:** Has repeatedly pushed replacement-theory-adjacent claims about Democrats using immigration to "fundamentally change the country."
- **Free speech hypocrisy:** Led the Murthy v. Missouri censorship case while supporting government efforts to pressure platforms on other content.

### Campaign Finance Red Flags

- A dark money group called "Conservatives Network" spent over $600,000 on electioneering ads supporting Schmitt during the 2022 primary. The Campaign Legal Center filed a complaint alleging the group illegally concealed its donors. The group had essentially no other activity.

### Social Media Behavior

Extremely active on X (@Eric_Schmitt). Posts frequently about government censorship, free speech, and immigration in combative, partisan framing. High engagement numbers.

### Suitability: 9.5/10

## #2: Denny Hoskins — Secretary of State

**Party:** Republican
**In office since:** January 2025
**Previous role:** State Senator
**National profile:** Medium — but his role makes him uniquely impactful

### Why He's a Strong Candidate

Hoskins occupies a uniquely powerful position: he is the official responsible for writing **neutral ballot summaries** that Missouri voters read before casting their votes. Courts have thrown out his ballot language **five times in just a few months** for being biased and misleading. His dishonesty directly affects whether voters understand what they're voting on.

### Key Controversies

- **Admitted in court** that he wrote ballot language "likely to create prejudice" against a redistricting referendum. His own attorney conceded the language was arguably "argumentative."
- **Courts rejected his ballot summaries 5 times** since October 2025, covering: redistricting, public education funding, private school vouchers, abortion rights, and the initiative petition process.
- **Falsely claimed** a petition to ban private school funding would "eliminate existing programs that provide direct aid to students with special education needs." The petition expressly protected those programs. An appeals court struck the language as misleading.
- **Characterized gaming the system as acceptable,** calling his misleading summaries a "first bite at the apple" — suggesting opponents could just sue if they objected.
- **Sued for defamation** after sharing an image on social media falsely identifying a man as an undocumented immigrant and shooter at the 2024 Kansas City parade shooting.
- **Missouri Republicans are now pushing legislation** to give Hoskins more power over ballot language and limit judicial oversight — creating a feedback loop of reduced accountability.

### Campaign Finance Red Flags

Standard Republican donor base. Detailed analysis through OpenSecrets warranted.

### Social Media Behavior

The defamation lawsuit over the false KC parade shooting post demonstrates willingness to share misleading content. Less active than federal-level politicians but the misleading posts he does share have real-world consequences.

### Suitability: 9/10

## #3: Mike Kehoe — Governor

**Party:** Republican
**In office since:** January 2025
**Previous role:** Lieutenant Governor
**National profile:** Medium-High as governor of a large state

### Why He's a Strong Candidate

As governor, Kehoe has the highest-profile state office and has already generated significant controversies around campaign finance, overturning voter-approved measures, and partisan redistricting. His relationship with mega-donor Rex Sinquefield raises serious questions about whose interests he serves.

### Key Controversies

- **Overturned voter-approved sick leave law:** Signed HB 567 in July 2025, repealing paid sick leave protections that Missouri voters passed at the ballot box.
- **Blamed outsiders for a ballot measure Missourians passed,** claiming Proposition A "was no one in Missouri who brought that bill to the ballot. It was bought and put on our ballot." The group behind it was Missouri-born and Missouri-funded.
- **Called a special session for partisan gerrymandering** with the explicit goal of ousting Democratic U.S. Rep. Emanuel Cleaver. A court filing raised questions about whether the gerrymandered map was prepared by his staff or outside operatives.
- **Signed a landlord discrimination law** allowing landlords to reject tenants using housing vouchers, Social Security Disability Insurance, child support, and tips.
- **Proposed $50M for private school scholarships** while underfunding the state education department.

### Campaign Finance Red Flags

- **Rex Sinquefield** (has donated $45M+ to mostly Republican campaigns) pumped at least $1.25 million into Kehoe's campaign through the American Dream PAC.
- Received donations from **Torch Electronics** (in a legal battle with the state over unregulated slot machines) and **Good Day Farms** (a cannabis company) after winning the primary.
- Rented a charter bus from **lobbyist Jewell Patek**, whose client Smithfield Foods is owned by a Chinese agricultural company — while campaigning on anti-China rhetoric.
- Total fundraising reached nearly $13 million by mid-2024.

### Social Media Behavior

Relatively restrained on social media. He reportedly considers "social-media spats to count for nothing compared to in-the-flesh relationships." Less of a social media accountability target, but strong for statement-vs-action tracking.

### Suitability: 8.5/10

## #4: Mark Alford — U.S. Representative (MO-4)

**Party:** Republican
**In office since:** 2023
**Previous role:** TV news anchor (WDAF-TV, Kansas City)
**National profile:** Medium — went viral multiple times in 2025

### Why He's a Good Candidate

Alford is a former TV journalist turned politician — a natural hypocrisy angle for someone who used to report the news and now generates misleading claims. He has generated national attention for contentious town halls, accusations of lying by his own constituents, and staff intimidation allegations.

### Key Controversies

- **"If you didn't want to hear my lies, why did you come here tonight?"** — Alford's sarcastic response to constituents chanting "liar" at an August 2025 town hall. This went viral nationally.
- **Accused of sending staff to intimidate a state lawmaker.** State Rep. David Tyson Smith said three Alford staffers demanded his office stop giving out Alford's official contact information to upset constituents. Alford denied it, calling the visit "relationship-building." Smith called him "the liar."
- **Suspended all public events and town halls** in September 2025, citing "this rise in political violence" — after facing sustained constituent anger over his support for Medicaid cuts.
- **Defended Trump's Medicaid cuts and National Guard deployment** at town halls while facing boos and tough questions from constituents.

### Campaign Finance Red Flags

Campaign raised $910,692 in 2025. Detailed donor analysis through OpenSecrets warranted.

### Social Media Behavior

Active on social media, but his more notable pattern is in-person confrontations going viral. His "lies" quote spread across X and cable news.

### Suitability: 7.5/10

## #5: Catherine Hanaway — Attorney General

**Party:** Republican
**In office since:** August 2025
**Previous role:** Speaker of the Missouri House; private attorney
**National profile:** Low-Medium

### Why She's a Good Candidate

Hanaway holds one of the most powerful state legal offices and has already been accused of making deliberate misrepresentations about the initiative petition process. Her prior work as a private attorney creates revolving-door conflict-of-interest angles.

### Key Controversies

- **Deliberately confused the initiative petition and referendum processes** in public statements. Critics called these "deliberate misrepresentations by elected officials who should know better."
- **Filed a federal lawsuit** claiming "out-of-state dark money groups" were "hijacking Missouri's electoral process" — targeting groups working on a redistricting referendum that Missourians were exercising their constitutional right to pursue.
- **Had to recuse herself** from the Grain Belt Express case (a wind power project she previously represented as lead counsel), calling the prior investigation "unlawful and politically motivated."
- **Filed a lawsuit against MSHSAA** over alleged discrimination, which MSHSAA characterized as "government overreach."

### Campaign Finance Red Flags

Rex Sinquefield was a major donor to the AG primary race. Same mega-donor ecosystem as Kehoe.

### Social Media Behavior

Less active on social media. Her accountability profile centers on official statements, legal filings, and the gap between stated positions and actions.

### Suitability: 6.5/10

## Why These Five Matter Together

Missouri is a case study in one-party dominance with minimal accountability infrastructure. The state's major newspapers have shrunk, local TV news is consolidating, and the politicians who control the state face almost no sustained fact-checking.

These five politicians collectively control:
- Both U.S. Senate seats (Hawley + Schmitt)
- The Governor's office (Kehoe)
- The Secretary of State / election integrity (Hoskins)
- The Attorney General / state legal enforcement (Hanaway)

A network of accountability sites covering this group would provide the kind of sustained scrutiny that Missouri's weakened local media can no longer deliver.
